## 1. Current Copyright Status

Your stories are **already copyrighted** under U.S. law. Copyright exists automatically the moment an original work is created and fixed in a tangible form — including digital publication. No registration, © symbol, or filing is required for basic protection.

The site footer already displays: `© {year} Gunner the Lab. All rights reserved.`

---

## 2. Why Formal Registration Matters

While copyright is automatic, **formal registration with the U.S. Copyright Office** unlocks critical legal benefits:

| Benefit | Details |
|---|---|
| **Lawsuit ability** | You **must** register before filing a copyright infringement lawsuit for U.S. works |
| **Statutory damages** | Registration before infringement (or within 3 months of publication) makes you eligible for statutory damages up to **$150,000 per work** + attorney's fees — without needing to prove actual financial loss |
| **Prima facie evidence** | Registration within 5 years of publication creates a legal presumption of validity in court |
| **Public record** | Creates an official record of your authorship and dates |

> **Note:** "Poor man's copyright" (mailing yourself a copy) has **no legal standing** and is not a substitute for registration.

## 3. Registration Options & Fees

Register at: **https://eco.copyright.gov** (the electronic Copyright Office)

| Method | Cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Single Application** (online) | **$35** | Single author, same claimant, one work, not work-for-hire — simplest option for individual stories |
| **Standard Application** (online) | **$55** | Required if work contains AI-generated content that needs to be disclaimed |
| **Paper filing** (Form TX) | **$85** | Literary works via mail — slowest option |

### Group Registration Options

- **GRUW** (Group Registration of Unpublished Works) — if any stories haven't been published yet
- **GRTX** (Group Registration for Short Online Literary Works) — designed for blog entries, short online articles, social media posts published online

At $55 per story (Standard Application), registering all 38 stories individually would cost **$2,090**. Group registration options could significantly reduce this.

## 4. AI-Generated Illustrations — The Big Complication

The U.S. Copyright Office issued an official **policy statement on March 16, 2023** ([Federal Register 88 FR 16190](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2023/03/16/2023-05321/copyright-registration-guidance-works-containing-material-generated-by-artificial-intelligence)) addressing works containing AI-generated content.

### Key Rules

| Content Type | Copyrightable? | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Human-written story text** | ✅ Yes | Fully copyrightable as original literary works |
| **AI-generated illustrations** | ❌ No | The Copyright Office considers AI-generated images to lack "human authorship" — the AI determines the expressive elements, not the human prompter |
| **Combined work** (text + AI images) | ✅ Partially | The work as a whole can be registered, but only the human-authored portions are protected |

### Registration Requirements for Works with AI Content

When registering stories that include AI-generated illustrations, you **must**:

1. Use the **Standard Application** ($55) — the $35 Single Application cannot be used
2. Describe what the human author created in the **"Author Created"** field (e.g., "text of the literary work")
3. **Explicitly disclaim** the AI-generated illustrations in the **"Limitation of the Claim" → "Material Excluded"** section (e.g., "Illustrations generated by artificial intelligence")
4. **Never** list the AI tool (Midjourney, DALL-E, etc.) as author or co-author

### The Zarya of the Dawn Precedent

The Copyright Office ruled that a graphic novel (*Zarya of the Dawn*) with human-authored text + Midjourney-generated images was copyrightable as a whole — the text and the human selection/arrangement were protected, but the individual AI-generated images were **not** independently protected.

This is the closest legal precedent to the Gunner the Lab stories (human text + AI illustrations).

## 5. LICENSE File Problem

The repo currently contains a **GNU General Public License v3 (GPL-3.0)**. This is a **software license** designed for open-source code — it explicitly grants everyone permission to copy, modify, and redistribute the work.

**This is the opposite of what you want for creative literary content.**

### Recommended Fix

Split licensing into two parts:

1. **Website code** (Astro components, configs, CSS, JavaScript): Keep an open-source license like MIT if desired
2. **Stories and illustrations** (everything in `src/content/stories/`, `resources/gunner-stories/`, `public/images/`): Use a restrictive license or "All Rights Reserved"

Options for the creative content:

| License | What It Allows |
|---|---|
| **All Rights Reserved** | No reuse without explicit permission — maximum protection |
| **CC BY-NC-ND 4.0** | Others can share (with attribution), but no commercial use and no modifications |
| **CC BY-NC 4.0** | Others can share and adapt (with attribution), but no commercial use |
