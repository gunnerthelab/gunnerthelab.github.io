## The Adventures of Gunner the Lab... Oh, and Tiger Too

This is a collection of mostly fictional, always fun stories about a black Lab named Gunner and his best friend Tiger, a tabby cat who would very much like you to know that he is *not* orange.

Gunner is the star. He lives for pets, snacks, and being in the exact right spot when food falls on the floor. He has a heart of gold and a brain that's... well, he tries. Tiger is the Brain to Gunner's Pinky — the schemer, the strategist, the one who would've taken over the world by now if his partner wasn't so easily distracted by a dropped piece of bacon.

The stories follow Gunner, Tiger, and their family — Dad, Mom, and three boys — across the homesteads, small towns, and backroads they've called home. From fifteen acres of East Texas mud and chickens, to a tiny garden plot they crammed an orchard into, to the mountains of Virginia where forty acres of woods finally gave a big black dog the room he always deserved to run.

Some of these stories are real. Some are very fictional. Most are somewhere in between — the kind of stories that start with "well, something *like* this happened" and end with everyone laughing, the dog covered in mud, and the cat looking deeply unimpressed.

## The Characters

**Gunner** — Black Labrador. Hunter safety orange collar. Star of every story, even the ones that aren't about him. Loves: pets, snacks, swimming, rolling in things he shouldn't, and any human willing to scratch behind his ears. Heroic in spirit, questionable in execution.

**Tiger** — Tabby cat. Gray and brown stripes. NOT orange. Demanding, strategic, secretly affectionate when he thinks nobody's looking. Survived the country, the coyotes, multiple moves, and every other cat that came and went. The brains of the entire operation. Will tolerate being held if milk is involved.

**Dad** — Cloud Architect by trade, farmer by stubbornness. Wears overalls. Keeps bees (or tries to). Has been outsmarted by both the dog and the cat on multiple occasions.

**Mom** — Ex-teacher turned homeschool mom. The busiest person in any room, any house, any state they've lived in. Keeps the whole operation running while everyone else, animals included, creates chaos around her. She rocks.

**The Oldest Boy** — The workhorse. Solid, dependable, tough, and smart. If something needs doing, he's already doing it. Builds things in the woodshop. Carries the quiet confidence of a kid who's been pulling his weight since before he could reach the workbench.

**The Middle Boy** — The dreamer. Imaginative, creative, sees the world a little differently than everyone else. Draws treasure maps. Builds secret forts. Probably has a whole kingdom mapped out in his head that nobody else knows about yet.

**The Youngest Boy** — Two speeds: on and off. First of the three to visit the ER. Has plans that involve wagons, hills, and zero adult supervision. When he's on, he is *on*. When he's off, he's asleep in ten seconds flat, usually on Gunner.

**Grandpa Bear** — The old brown mutt who came before. Grumpy. Didn't like anyone in his yard. Barely tolerated the family, let alone a bouncing black Lab puppy who wouldn't stop trying to play with him. But a tiny tabby kitten once slept on his back, and he let it happen. That tells you everything you need to know about Bear.

## About the Author

These stories are written by Dad — a Cloud Architect who spends his days building things in the sky and his evenings watching a black Lab and a tabby cat prove, over and over again, that the best partnerships are the ones nobody planned.

The family has lived on homesteads in East Texas, squeezed onto a single acre near Waco, and now calls the southwestern Virginia mountains home. Through every move, every new piece of land, every flock of chickens and swarm of bees, two things have stayed constant: Gunner's appetite and Tiger's complete refusal to be impressed by anything.

These stories are for the family, for the boys, and for anyone who's ever watched a dog do something spectacularly unhelpful and loved him more for it.
