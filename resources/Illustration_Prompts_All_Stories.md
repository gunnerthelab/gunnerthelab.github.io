## AI IMAGE GENERATION TIPS

### For Consistent Results Across All Illustrations:

1. **Copy the full prompt** for each scene — character descriptions are embedded so the AI sees them every time. Do not shorten the character descriptions between prompts.

2. **Gunner consistency keywords**: "large 90-pound black Labrador Retriever, jet-black coat, broad muscular chest, blocky English-Lab head, amber-brown eyes, hunter-safety-orange nylon collar" — use this exact phrasing every time.

3. **Tiger consistency keywords**: "stocky gray-brown tabby cat, dark classic tabby stripes on lighter gray-brown base, golden-amber eyes, NOT orange" — the "NOT orange" is critical. AI models default to making tabby cats orange.

4. **Dad consistency keywords**: "large heavyset man, full thick dark brown bushy beard, tan wide-brimmed safari hat, blue denim Dickies overalls with brass buttons over gray t-shirt" — all of these details every time.

5. **The truck never changes**: "well-used dark blue full-size American pickup truck, crew cab, faded paint, minor dents" — same truck in every scene it appears.

6. **Boys age by era**: Check the age chart above and use the correct era token for each story. Don't mix Virginia-age boys into East Texas scenes.

7. **Art style at the start**: Always begin the prompt with the art style — "Graphite pencil illustration, crosshatched, softly shaded black and white" or "Colored pencil illustration, warm hand-drawn textured style."

8. **Quality tags at the end**: Always close with "Storybook illustration, children's book quality, detailed professional illustration, hand-drawn, 3:2 landscape."

9. **Negative prompts** (if supported): "no photorealism, no digital painting, no anime, no watercolor, no orange tabby cat"

10. **Aspect ratio**: 3:2 landscape for all scene illustrations. 2:3 portrait only for character close-ups.

11. **Reference images**: If the tool supports image references, upload the real Gunner and Tiger photos for character consistency. The snow photo (`IMG_0405.JPEG`) is the definitive Gunner reference. The family East Texas photo (`family_east_texas_2021.jpg`) is the definitive family reference.

### Filename Convention

All filenames follow this pattern:
```
story-XX-scene-YY-short-description.png
```
- `XX` = two-digit story number (01–38)
- `YY` = two-digit scene number (01, 02, 03...)
- `short-description` = 2–4 word kebab-case description
- Extension: `.png` (change to `.jpg` if using photographic-style generation)

Place generated images in: `public/images/stories/`
