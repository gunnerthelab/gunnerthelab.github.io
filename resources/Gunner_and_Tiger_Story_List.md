# The Adventures of Gunner the Lab... Oh, and Tiger Too

## Official Story List

## The Series

Mostly fictional, always fun stories about **Gunner**, a black lab, and his best friend **Tiger**, a tabby cat. Gunner is the star. Tiger is the Brain to Gunner's Pinky — the schemer, the strategist, the one who would run the world if his partner wasn't so easily distracted by snacks.

## Art Style

Each story should include illustrations in a **classic storybook illustration** style:
- **Graphite pencil illustration** — hand-drawn, crosshatched, softly shaded black and white pencil art (ideal for flashback stories and heart stories)
- **Colored pencil illustration** — warm, textured, hand-drawn color art (ideal for current-day Virginia stories and adventures)
- Reference photos of Gunner and Tiger are available in the project files for accuracy (Gunner: black lab with hunter safety orange collar; Tiger: tabby cat, NOT orange)

## The Beginning

**1. The Voyage Home, Going East** ✅ *WRITTEN*
Before there was Gunner, there was Bear — and before there was a homestead in East Texas, there was a decision made on a bench in an apple orchard outside Phoenix. The family loads up two vehicles and drives eighteen hundred miles east into a new life.

**2. Donkeys, Ducks, and One More Thing** ✅ *WRITTEN*
The family's first summer on fifteen acres. Chickens, ducks, and two donkeys named Mr. Snickers and Beep fill up the homestead fast. Bear becomes the reluctant supervisor of all of it. But Dad is keeping a quiet secret — he thinks the place could use one more thing.

## East Texas Flashbacks (The Homestead Days — 15 Acres)

**3. Cow Tipping Point**
Young Gunner tries to befriend the cows. The cows are not interested. Tiger watches from a fence post and narrates like a nature documentary.

**4. The Chicken Caper** ✅ *WRITTEN*
Chickens and ducks escape the coop. Gunner charges in to help and makes everything spectacularly worse — ending up soaked in the pond while the ducks look smug. Tiger herds three chickens back into the coop without moving more than five feet. Nobody is surprised.

**5. Grandpa Bear's Last Stand**
A warm, funny tribute to old Bear — the grumpy brown mutt who didn't like anyone in his yard. Puppy Gunner keeps trying to play with him. Kitten Tiger sleeps through the whole thing on Bear's back.

**6. Duck, Duck, Gunner**
The ducks have taken over the pond and Gunner is sent to reclaim it. He ends up swimming laps with them instead. Tiger's plan B involves a garden hose.

**7. The Fifteen-Acre Kingdom**
Tiger's origin story. How a tiny tabby kitten born on a Texas homestead decided that the big goofy black dog was, reluctantly, the best warm pillow in the world.

**8. The Beekeeper's Apprentice**
The family gets their first bee hives. Gunner is fascinated and gets way too close. Tiger watches from a safe distance, tail twitching, as Gunner learns the hard way that bees are not his friends. Dad in full bee suit trying to keep Gunner away becomes a recurring bit.

**9. The Hive Mind**
The bees have settled in and Tiger has developed a respectful distance. Gunner still hasn't learned. When one hive gets agitated, Tiger tries to warn Gunner with every cat signal he's got. Gunner interprets this as playtime.

**10. Honey Heist**
Harvest day. The smell of fresh honey fills the air. Tiger devises a plan to get a taste. Gunner doesn't need a plan — he just shoves his face where it doesn't belong. The boys try to help Dad extract honey while keeping both animals out of the operation. Nobody succeeds.

**11. The Great Cookie Caper** ✅ *WRITTEN*
Mom's out of town. Dad and the boys bake store-bought cookies, eat half, and leave the rest on the kitchen island. Next morning: empty plate. Nobody confesses. The mystery goes unsolved for weeks — Dad thinks he cleaned them up, the boys get guilt-tripped, Mom is told the story and laughs it off. Nobody suspects the dog. Weeks later, Mom and the boys are gone again, and Dad bakes a *real* batch from scratch. He hears something suspicious from down the hall, creeps back into the kitchen, and catches Gunner fully upright on his back legs, front paws on the island, tongue stretched across to the cooling rack like a pink, slobbery bridge of desperate hope. Tiger, on top of the refrigerator, knew the whole time. Tiger doesn't testify. Cats are like that.

## The Big Moves (Road Trip / Transition Stories)

**12. The Great Migration**
The family packs up the East Texas homestead. Gunner thinks they're going to the vet. Tiger knows exactly what's happening and has opinions. A chaotic moving-day story.

**13. Gholson Gardens**
Tiger and Gunner adjust to one acre after fifteen. Tiger claims the entire garden as his territory. Gunner keeps digging up what Mom just planted. The orchard becomes their new adventure zone.

**14. The Long Drive East**
The family moves to Virginia. Tiger rides in a crate like royalty. Gunner has his head out the window for 800 miles. Told from both perspectives.

**15. New Territory**
First day on 40 acres in Virginia. Gunner marks every single tree (it takes a while). Tiger finds the highest point on the property and declares himself lord of the mountain.

**16. The Invisible Fence**
China Springs, TX. No good fence, so the family buys an electric fence. Gunner gets his first zap and bolts to Dad like a terrified puppy. For days he won't go outside, standing at the door trembling, looking at the yard like it personally betrayed him. Tiger strolls in and out freely (cats don't wear the collar) with maximum smugness. The boys try to coax Gunner out with treats. He wants the treats. He does not want the yard. The internal conflict nearly breaks him. Eventually he inches out one paw at a time like he's crossing a minefield.

## The Virginia Homestead (Current Setting — 40 Acres)

**17. The Great Acorn Heist**
Tiger devises a plan to raid the squirrels' acorn stash. Gunner's job: be the distraction. Gunner gets distracted by the distraction.

**18. 40 Acres of Trouble**
Gunner discovers a mysterious trail into the hundred acres of woods next door. Tiger reluctantly follows to keep him alive. They find something unexpected.

**19. The Night Shift**
Strange sounds from the woods at night. Tiger investigates with cool composure from the windowsill. Gunner barks at his own reflection. Turns out it's just opossums having a party.

**20. Mountain Dog, Valley Cat**
The first Virginia winter hits hard. Gunner is thrilled (snow!). Tiger is horrified (snow.). Two very different approaches to the same cold day.

**21. The Porch Bandit**
Something keeps stealing food off the porch. Tiger sets traps. Gunner eats the bait. Every. Single. Time. The actual culprit — a raccoon — watches from the treeline, entertained.

**22. The Grill Master's First Case** ✅ *WRITTEN*
The youngest boy earns his first official night as Grill Master — five perfect T-bone steaks, cooked to perfection on the Virginia deck. He ducks inside for fifteen seconds to grab foil and comes back to an empty plate. Gunner is lying six feet away, pretending to be a rug. No scraps. No bones. No evidence. Just one ninety-pound Labrador with a suspiciously full stomach and literal steak evidence still in the back of his mouth. Dad declares it a four-paw review and the highest honor a dog can give a cook. Dinner becomes grilled cheese. Mom laughs until she has to sit on the floor. The youngest ends the night with Gunner's head on his knee under the stars: "You're a bad dog." Gunner's tail thumps. He knows.

**23. The Bagel Bandit** ✅ *WRITTEN*
Mom comes home with store-bought bagels — a rare and sacred household event. The middle boy sets the bag on the porch steps for one second. Gunner, lying in the grass looking casual, covers the distance in two bounds and makes a run for the woods — bag in mouth, tail up, committed. Then the oldest steps out of the woodshop and delivers a single command in a voice that stops a ninety-pound Lab dead in his tracks. The bagels drop unharmed. Gunner melts into the grass in full-body pout. The middle boy cracks in forty-five seconds and provides ear scratches. The oldest goes back to the woodshop in disgust. On top of the fridge, Tiger watches it all and decides the dog has talent — raw, unrefined, solo talent. What he needs is a partner with a plan.

**39. Shotgun** ✅ *WRITTEN*
The oldest gets his learner's permit and Dad climbs into the passenger seat for the first time in nine years with one beautiful thought: *I never have to drive again.* But Gunner has been the shotgun dog for nine years across three states, and he is not giving up that seat without a fight. Tiger, who hasn't voluntarily entered a vehicle in nine years, clears his schedule to watch.

**40. The Gentlemen Next Door** ✅ *WRITTEN*
Something large has moved into the neighbors' field — three enormous Suffolk Punch draft horses with kind eyes and a warm rolling dialect Gunner can't understand a word of. Tiger, naturally, has already been over there twice and made friends. A story about meeting gentle giants and learning that sometimes the nicest things you'll ever hear are the things you don't quite understand.

## The Boys & Family Stories

**24. Ben's Big Idea**
The youngest boy (two speeds: on and off) has a plan involving Gunner, a wagon, a hill, and zero adult supervision. Tiger sees it coming and tries to warn everyone by sitting directly in the path and glaring.

**25. The Woodshop Apprentice**
The oldest boy is building something in the shop. Gunner "helps" by laying on every tool. Tiger keeps batting screws off the workbench. The project gets done despite them.

**26. Mom's Day Off**
Mom tries to have a quiet morning. Tiger demands milk. Gunner demands pets. The boys need approximately forty-seven things. Gunner and Tiger accidentally team up to give her five minutes of peace — then immediately ruin it.

**27. Dad's Cloud**
The boys ask Dad what he does for work. Dad tries to explain cloud architecture. Gunner thinks they're talking about actual clouds. Tiger looks out the window at the sky, deeply unimpressed.

**28. The Dreamer's Map**
The middle boy draws an elaborate treasure map of the property. Gunner and Tiger follow it. It leads to the middle boy's secret fort in the woods.

## Seasonal / Holiday Stories

**29. The Christmas Ham Incident**
Tiger devises his most ambitious plan yet: steal Christmas dinner. Gunner is recruited as muscle. The family catches them mid-heist. Gunner's face says sorry. Tiger's face shows zero remorse.

**30. The Fourth of July**
Fireworks terrify Gunner. Tiger couldn't care less. A story about Tiger actually comforting Gunner, curling up beside him, even though he'd never admit it.

**31. The First Snow**
Based on real events and real photos. Gunner sits in the snow like a majestic statue. Tiger steps in it once, shakes his paw in disgust, and goes back inside. Then comes back out because Gunner won't come in.

**32. The Garden Heist of Spring**
Something is eating Mom's seedlings. Gunner is accused. Tiger is accused. It's actually deer, but Tiger frames Gunner anyway.

---

## Adventure Stories

**33. Operation: Mailman**
Tiger becomes convinced the mailman is a spy. He recruits Gunner for a stakeout. Gunner loves the mailman. This creates significant operational conflict.

**34. The Creature in the Creek**
Gunner finds something weird in the creek on the property. Tiger analyzes it from a safe distance. It's a snapping turtle. Nobody wins.

**35. When Gunner Met the Bear**
A real black bear wanders onto the Virginia property. Gunner thinks it's a big dog. Tiger knows better. Tiger saves the day through sheer strategic brilliance and a well-timed hiss from high ground.

## Heart Stories

**36. The One About Grandpa**
A quieter, warmer story. Gunner remembers Dad's dad — the man who always had treats and a slow hand for petting. Tiger watched from across the room. A tribute told through the animals' eyes.

**37. Why Tiger Stayed**
All the other cats came and went. Tiger survived the country, the moves, the dogs, the coyotes, all of it. This is the story of why — and it has everything to do with a big, dumb, warm black dog who never once chased him.

**38. The Long Walk Home** ✅ *WRITTEN*
Bear's eyes go cloudy. His hearing fades. The world gets quieter and blurrier, but the grumpy old alpha is too proud to ask for help. So Gunner gives it without being asked — walking beside Bear on hikes, guiding him back when he wanders, waiting at the porch steps so Bear can find his way up. The big goofy dog who couldn't herd a chicken becomes a seeing-eye dog nobody trained. A story about showing up, staying close, and the quiet love between two dogs who never quite understood each other but never left each other's side.

- **Family**: Dad (Cloud Architect), Mom (homeschool mom, ex-teacher, busiest person alive), three boys (names TBD — oldest is the workhorse, middle is the dreamer, youngest has two speeds: on and off).
